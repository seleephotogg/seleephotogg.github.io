Toronto's Cavalcade of Lights

Cavalcade of Lights is one of the big festival happen in Toronto.

Cavalcade of Lights which is festival events has been there for nearly 50 years. There are illumination (or light display) of Toronto's Christmas Trees, performacne, musical talents, DJ Skating party, and firework.(2,3)

This year, the event held in last month (November 26, 2016) at the Nathan Phillips Squares. (1) The event start with 7pm with choir performance. (3)

Here is the schedule in that day,
Here.

Schedule:

7 p.m.

Audience sing-a-long with Choir! Choir! Choir! including John Lennon's Imagine with special guests, Melissa McClelland and Luke Doucet.

Choir! Choir! Choir! This ultra-inclusive choir leads the audience into a joyous communal experience singing contemporary pop music. Listen, or join in on the fun!

7:30 p.m.



 First lighting of Toronto’s Official Christmas Tree sponsored by Hudson's Bay and powered by Toronto Hydro.

7:35 p.m.

Cris Derksen's Cavalcade Orchestra:

Featuring the Chippewa Travellers, Raven Kanatakta, Jesse Baird, Dr. Draw, Jessica Deutsch, Megan Hodge and Michael Fedyshun with guest vocalists Don Amero, Dan Talevski and Kiran Ahluwalia.

Cris Derksen  Known for her multi-layered sound, JUNO-nominated Derksen combines classical music. Her aboriginal ancestry and new-school electronics, create genre-defying music. Cris Derksen is the musical director for the Cavalcade Orchestra.
Don Amero  Melting hearts from coast to coast with his silky vocals, inviting stage presence and soulful percussive guitar.
Dan Talevski   One of the original YouTube stars and MUCH Digital creator. Check out Talevski's pop, dance and R&B influenced music.
Kiran Ahluwalia  This two-time JUNO award-winning singer-songwriter creates a hybrid of the contemporary and traditional, incorporating vocal traditions of India and Pakistan.

8:30 p.m.

Procession of Light by Red Pepper Spectacle Arts.

Red Pepper Spectacle Arts Interweaving arts with an aboriginal flair, this artist group will illuminate Nathan Philips Square with a procession of giant puppets, costumed characters, a street band, stilt walkers and fire artists.

8:35 p.m.


 Spectacular fireworks display.

8:40 p.m.

Whitehorse Concert sponsored by Great Gulf.
Whitehorse

JUNO award-winning husband-and-wife duo Luke Doucet and Melissa McClelland tie the musical knot! They use extensive live looping, with percussion, and acoustic, electric and bass guitars.

9:40 - 10:30 p.m.

Skating party with DJ General Eclectic.

DJ General Eclectic This DJ truly lives up to his name as one of Canada's most diverse DJs, taking you on a musical journey around the globe and back again with the flick of his wrist!
