Gimbap (Korean Sushi)

Untitled Document

Gimbap

What is Gimbap?

From Wikipedia(1),
"Gimbap or kimbap is a Korean dish[1] made from steamed white rice (bap) and various other ingredients, rolled in gim (sheets of dried laver seaweed) and served in bite-size slices.[2] Gimbap is often eaten during picnics or outdoor events, or as a light lunch, served with takuan or kimchi.[3]"

Today in this post, I am, yes, try to make Gimbap. I will show steps by steps.

Step 1, First prepare the rice, seaweed, and side-dish that you want to put inside the seaweed and rice. For me, I just purchased the side-dish in the nearby supermarket.

Step 2. Now once you prepare all of them, put your seaweed. I don't have the wrapper with me. So I use the aluminium foil.

Step 3. Now, put your rice on the seaweed. Make sure, you spread the rice widely.

Step 4. Now, put your side-dish on to the rice. For reference, for Japanese sushi/roll, you put rice first.

Step 5, now, wrap the roll up.
